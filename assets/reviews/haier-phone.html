Haier Elegance Review
16 / 03 / 2010

Long story short: old phone had a stroke and perished. Found a nice dude who sold me this oversized tampon-shaped phone from the back of his car. Complete package: chargers, headset and cables, included a 1GB microSD card; total price: $30. Excellent bargain.

It makes calls.

Size: miniscule
Battery life: excellent
Features: just enough
Time to learn: minutes
Overall: fantastico!


The cellphone itself is incredibly small. As long as an index finger and as wide as a US quarter. As some might say, good things come in small packages. I intend to agree. The Haier Elegance has everything you could ever want built in: an MP3 player, an FM radio, 64MB of phone storage, and a microSD slot for mass storage.



A quick inspection shows how well designed and put together it is. There are no plastic snap-off edge remnants, no proprietary connectors to charge the phone and no weird headphone connectors that force you to buy extra accessories just to be able to use a headset. The mini-USB port takes care of both charging the phone's 830 mAh battery and loading files onto the device. It's compatible with any OS and shows up as just another flash storage device. The headphone port detects when you've plugged something in and changes the volume accordingly.

The MP3 player is basic but it gets the job done very well. Driving around in Hamilton, I can easily load up my recent flavors of tracks into the phone and connect the phone to the car with the household 3.5mm cable. You change and pause tracks with the three silver buttons on the keypad.

It takes about a day to really get used to the interface of the phone. A rocker switch on the right side helps you get around faster in the menus. Should you want to dial down the brightness on the screen or change other settings, you'll find that there aren't any extra menus or keypresses needed to do so. It's clear from using the Elegance that someone optimized the flow of the interface. One flick and a push on the rocker switch puts you straight into typing a text message, two clicks and you'll be in your contact list. Dead simple.

Speaker volume and microphone quality is good enough to make calls in a crowded room. Reception is decent.
However, battery is hard to find (just open up the old battery guts / roll your own) and they're out of production (~2008). But you should quite easily come by these secondhand. All in all, highly recommended!
